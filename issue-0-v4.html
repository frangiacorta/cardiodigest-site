ANTITHROMBOTIC STRATEGY AFTER M-TEER — STAR-TEER

Phase 4 trial enrolling patients with mitral regurgitation undergoing transcatheter edge-to-edge repair without an indication for oral anticoagulation, addressing the unresolved question of optimal post-procedural antithrombotic strategy. Results will directly inform practice for the growing M-TEER population. ClinicalTrials.gov ↗

LEVOSIMENDAN IN CARDIOGENIC SHOCK — Phase 3 Morbidity-Mortality Trial

Phase 3 trial evaluating whether early add-on levosimendan on top of conventional inotrope use reduces a combined morbidity-mortality endpoint in cardiogenic shock. This is one of the few adequately powered RCTs targeting inotrope strategy in this high-mortality syndrome. ClinicalTrials.gov ↗

EMPAGLIFLOZIN IN CARDIOGENIC SHOCK — EMPA-SHOCK Phase 3

Phase 3 trial examining 3-month effects of early empagliflozin initiation in cardiogenic shock on mortality, rehospitalisation, LVEF, and renal function — the first SGLT2 inhibitor trial specifically targeting the acute cardiogenic shock phenotype, complementing the emerging EMPA-CS signal. ClinicalTrials.gov ↗

CORTICOSTEROIDS IN INFLAMMATION-ENRICHED HF CARDIOGENIC SHOCK — CORTISHOCK-P

Phase 2 trial of corticosteroids in heart failure cardiogenic shock with high inflammatory burden, targeting a phenotype where immunomodulation may attenuate cytokine-driven haemodynamic deterioration. A positive signal could reshape the management algorithm for inflammatory-predominant cardiogenic shock. ClinicalTrials.gov ↗

VULNERABLE PLAQUE PCI — Interventional Therapy for Non-Flow-Limiting Vulnerable Plaques

Phase 4 trial assessing the effectiveness of PCI for non-flow-limiting but imaging-identified vulnerable plaques, directly challenging the conservative paradigm and addressing a major unmet need in stabilising high-risk coronary lesions. ClinicalTrials.gov ↗

Tier 1

Practice-changing

— — Life-Threatening Delayed Myocardial Ischaemia, Ventricular Arrhythmias, and Sudden Cardiac Death After Pulsed-Field Ablation of AF
Gunawardene MA et al. · JACC Clin Electrophysiol 2026 · primary source ↗
Structured abstract
Background
Pulsed-field ablation (PFA) has been adopted rapidly due to its non-thermal mechanism and apparent safety advantages over radiofrequency and cryoablation. However, rare but life-threatening ischaemic and arrhythmic adverse events occurring after PFA delivery have been incompletely characterised, with no systematic synthesis of available evidence.
Methods
Extended case series and systematic literature review including patients with atrial fibrillation who experienced ischaemic adverse events with delayed onset or arrhythmic adverse events after PFA energy delivery between March 2023 and January 2026. Events were classified as: (1) delayed and/or recurrent ST-segment elevation; (2) ventricular arrhythmia; or (3) sudden cardiac death. Nine newly published patients came from 5,963 procedures (0.15%) at the authors' centre; 30 additional cases were identified from literature review.
Key results
A total of 39 patients were included (mean age 67 ± 10 years; 72% men; 72% persistent AF; 51% coronary artery disease). Seventeen patients presented with delayed ST-segment elevation at a median of 16 minutes (IQR 4–315 minutes) after last PFA delivery; of these, 11 (65%) subsequently developed ventricular arrhythmia and 2 died (12%). Among the 13 patients with available 12-lead ECGs, ST elevation was inferior in 10 (77%), anteroseptal in 1 (8%), and both in 2 (15%). Ventricular arrhythmia occurred in a further 6 patients. Sudden cardiac death occurred in 16 patients at a median of 6 days (IQR 2–18 days) after PFA.
Conclusion
Rare but life-threatening ischaemic and malignant arrhythmic adverse events associated with PFA underscore the need for structured post-procedural surveillance and standardised adverse event reporting. Understanding underlying mechanisms is essential to identify patients at risk.
Critical appraisal
Study design
Prospective single-centre case series plus systematic literature review. The 0.15% incidence reflects only reported and documented cases — true population frequency may differ. No denominator for the literature review cases, limiting overall rate estimates. Central event classification enhances consistency.
Clinical relevance
PFA is undergoing rapid adoption globally with thousands of procedures now performed weekly. Even at 0.15%, if the incidence is reproducible across institutions, the absolute number of patients at risk is substantial. The 6-day median to sudden cardiac death means events can occur after hospital discharge, a window where structured monitoring protocols do not yet exist.
Context
Multiple mechanisms have been proposed for coronary injury during PFA, including direct thermal effects from bubble generation, coronary spasm, and phrenic nerve injury. The high proportion of inferior ST elevation raises the possibility of right coronary artery involvement. The 51% prevalence of coronary artery disease in affected patients is significantly higher than typical AF ablation populations, suggesting a possible pre-existing vulnerability.
Limitations
Case series design with ascertainment bias; events from a period of learning curve and evolving device configurations; PFA system heterogeneity across cases; inability to establish causal relationship or population-level rate; coronary anatomy data incomplete in most cases.
Bottom line: PFA-related delayed ischaemia and sudden cardiac death are rare but real — the field urgently needs standardised post-procedure surveillance protocols and adverse event registries as PFA becomes the dominant ablation modality.
ReTAVI — Early Outcomes of Redo-TAVI with the SAPIEN 3 Platform: Prospective Multicentre Registry
Tarantini G et al. · EuroIntervention 2026 · primary source ↗
Structured abstract
Background
TAVI is increasingly performed in younger, lower-risk patients who may outlive their transcatheter heart valve. Redo-TAVI procedures are expected to rise, yet real-world evidence on procedural safety and efficacy across diverse failed valve types remains limited.
Methods
Prospective, international study enrolling consecutive patients with a failed aortic transcatheter heart valve undergoing redo-TAVI with a balloon-expandable SAPIEN 3 THV across 59 international centres, with central imaging analysis and independent clinical event adjudication.
Key results
143 patients were enrolled (median age 84 years; 40.6% female; median STS risk score 7.0%). Predominant failing THVs were CoreValve/Evolut (53.1%), SAPIEN 3 (30.1%), and ACURATE (14.0%). Most common failure mode was isolated regurgitation (48.6%), followed by stenosis (35.2%) and mixed pathology (16.2%). Technical success was achieved in 95.1%. Thirty-day mortality was 3.5%; stroke 0.7%; pacemaker implantation 6.3%. Redo-TAVI significantly improved haemodynamics: mean gradients decreased overall (Δ=−12.0 mmHg) and more markedly in stenotic failures (Δ=−29.0 mmHg). Coronary obstruction occurred in 1.4% of cases.
Conclusion
Redo-TAVI with a balloon-expandable SAPIEN 3 platform is a safe and effective reintervention strategy across diverse failed THV types when guided by the Heart Team, standardised procedural planning, and comprehensive imaging.
Critical appraisal
Study design
Prospective multicentre registry with central imaging and independent event adjudication — highest quality real-world evidence design below an RCT. No randomised surgical comparator limits head-to-head interpretation.
Clinical relevance
With the first TAVI wave now ageing into bioprosthetic failure, redo-TAVI is transitioning from niche to expected practice. A 30-day mortality of 3.5% in octogenarians with 7.0% STS risk is comparable to index TAVI outcomes in similar risk cohorts, validating the procedure.
Context
The 1.4% coronary obstruction rate and 6.3% pacemaker rate, both in a highly planned multicentre setting, represent the best achievable outcomes under systematic CT-guided planning and leaflet modification strategies — confirming that the procedural learning curve is steep and centralisation matters.
Limitations
Observational design without surgical comparator; 30-day outcomes only — long-term valve durability within confined neo-annular dimensions is unknown and critical for lifetime management planning. Centre heterogeneity in planning protocols cannot be fully adjusted.
Bottom line: Redo-TAVI with SAPIEN 3 achieves 95.1% technical success and 3.5% 30-day mortality across diverse failed valve types — a viable strategy requiring CT-guided planning and Heart Team coordination at experienced centres.
GeminiOne-DMR — Novel Transcatheter Edge-to-Edge Repair System for Degenerative Mitral Regurgitation: 1-Year Outcomes
Zhu D et al. · JACC Asia 2026 · primary source ↗
Structured abstract
Background
For patients with severe degenerative mitral regurgitation (DMR) at prohibitive or high surgical risk, transcatheter edge-to-edge repair (TEER) is an established alternative to surgery. The GeminiOne is a novel TEER device featuring a unique groove-cam mechanism, with limited evidence on 1-year outcomes.
Methods
Prospective single-arm trial (GeminiOne-DMR) enrolling 120 patients with prohibitive surgical risk and DMR grade ≥3+. All patients were evaluated by an independent echocardiography core laboratory and a clinical event committee. The primary endpoint was clinical success, defined as freedom from all-cause mortality, mitral valve reintervention, and MR >2+ at 1-year follow-up.
Key results
At 1 year, the clinical success rate was 89.84% ± 2.78% (95% CI 84.56%–95.46%), meeting the prespecified primary efficacy endpoint. Freedom from 1-year all-cause mortality was 97.48% ± 1.35%; freedom from major adverse events 91.61% ± 2.54%; freedom from mitral valve reintervention 98.31% ± 1.02%; freedom from HF hospitalisation 94.00% ± 2.20%. MR ≤2+ was achieved in 92.24% of patients at 1 year. The proportion of patients in NYHA class I/II increased from 23.33% at baseline to 94.74% at 1 year (P<0.001). Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire scores also improved significantly from baseline to 1 year (P<0.001).
Conclusion
The GeminiOne TEER system demonstrated initial safety and efficacy for treating significant symptomatic DMR in patients at prohibitive or high surgical risk, with high clinical success at 1 year and marked functional improvement.
Critical appraisal
Study design
Prospective single-arm trial with independent core lab and event committee adjudication — robust evidence for a first-in-human/pivotal single-arm design. No comparator arm (randomised MitraClip/PASCAL comparison not reported). Sample size (n=120) appropriate for regulatory pathway but limits subgroup power.
Clinical relevance
Clinical success of 89.84% at 1 year and near-complete NYHA class normalisation (94.74% in class I/II at 1 year from 23.33% baseline) represents a clinically meaningful benefit comparable to established TEER platforms. The groove-cam mechanism aims to maintain clip stability while accommodating diverse leaflet morphologies.
Context
The global TEER market is dominated by MitraClip and PASCAL. Competitive devices validated with rigorous trial data expand procedural options, particularly in complex DMR anatomy where existing clip designs may suboptimally grasp leaflets. The 92.24% MR ≤2+ rate aligns with published MitraClip registries, establishing non-inferiority context.
Limitations
Single-arm design without direct comparison to established TEER devices; single-country enrolment potentially limiting generalisability; longer-term valve durability data needed; functional improvement may partly reflect case selection in a prohibitive-risk population with limited baseline activity.
Bottom line: GeminiOne TEER achieves 89.84% clinical success and 97.48% 1-year survival in prohibitive-risk DMR, with NYHA class I/II improving from 23% to 95% — a validated new structural device expanding the TEER toolkit.
— — Mavacamten in Obstructive vs Non-Obstructive HCM: Meta-Analysis of 5 RCTs
Alazzam AY et al. · Heart 2026 · source ↗

This meta-analysis of 5 RCTs (1,083 patients; 444 oHCM, 639 nHCM) assessed mavacamten's efficacy and safety stratified by HCM phenotype. In obstructive HCM, mavacamten markedly improved NYHA class (OR 4.94; 95% CI 3.25–7.49) and KCCQ-CSS (MD 8.99), with substantial LVOT gradient reductions; serious adverse events did not increase overall. In non-obstructive HCM, the risk of LVEF declining below 50% was considerably elevated (OR 14.35; 95% CI 5.92–34.77), and nHCM patients showed no functional benefit, with a suggestive KCCQ-CSS worsening (MD −0.70; 95% CI −1.26 to −0.14). Biomarkers (NT-proBNP, hs-cTnI) decreased substantially across both phenotypes. These data formally establish the phenotype boundary for mavacamten use and support restricting it to obstructive disease outside clinical trials.

— — TAVI and Coronary Interventions: Indications, Technical Considerations, and Clinical Scenarios
Aquino Bruno H et al. · EuroIntervention 2026 · source ↗

This state-of-the-art EuroIntervention review addresses the full spectrum of coronary-TAVI interaction, covering physiological assessment, PCI timing before and after valve implantation, and management of coronary obstruction. Anatomical and device-related factors including THV design, aortic root anatomy, and commissural alignment receive particular attention. The review provides a structured decision-making framework for the increasingly common scenario of combined valve and coronary disease requiring sequential or simultaneous treatment.

— — Sex-Specific and Time-Dependent Outcomes After TAVR vs SAVR: Meta-Analysis of Nine RCTs
Hendrianus H et al. · JACC Adv 2026 · source ↗

This meta-analysis pooled nine RCTs (9,583 patients; 44.2% women) with sex-stratified mortality-inclusive endpoints. In women, TAVR was associated with lower risk at 1 year (RR 0.67; 95% CI 0.55–0.81) and 2 years (RR 0.80; 95% CI 0.69–0.94), but no significant difference emerged at 5 years (RR 1.03; 95% CI 0.89–1.18); in men, no significant differences were observed across time points, and a numerically higher risk with TAVR was observed at 5 years in intermediate-high-risk men. These data support explicit inclusion of sex and time horizon in heart team discussions about TAVR versus SAVR.

— — Statins and Survival Free of Incident Frailty Among Older US Veterans
Qazi S et al. · Eur Heart J 2026 · source ↗

In a propensity-score-weighted analysis of 987,301 older US veterans (mean age 72 years) including 290,729 statin initiators, statin use was associated with a significantly lower risk of incident frailty or death over a mean follow-up of 5.3 years (HR 0.76; 95% CI 0.75–0.76); a comparable benefit was observed in pre-frail veterans at baseline. The unadjusted event rates were 153.1 vs 111.4 per 1,000 person-years (initiators vs non-initiators), with the protective association surviving propensity weighting. This adds frailty prevention to the well-established cardiovascular benefits of statins and may inform prescribing decisions in older patients at cardiovascular risk.

— — SGLT2 Inhibition Reduces Incident Heart Failure in Cardiomyopathy Genetic Variant Carriers
Marston NA et al. · Nat Med 2026 · source ↗

In this pharmacogenomic analysis of whole-exome sequencing data from the DECLARE-TIMI 58 trial (12,685 patients), 121 carried a pathogenic or likely pathogenic cardiomyopathy variant. Over a median follow-up of 4.2 years, dapagliflozin reduced hospitalisation for HF more strongly in carriers (HR 0.18; 95% CI 0.04–0.86) than non-carriers (HR 0.70; 95% CI 0.57–0.86; P interaction 0.03). The absolute risk reduction was 13.0% in carriers vs 1.0% in non-carriers; 82% of carriers had no prior HF, and in carriers without prior HF the absolute risk reduction was 12.8% vs 0.6%. These data raise the possibility that SGLT2 inhibition should be started early as primary HF prevention in genotype-positive individuals.

FAVOR III China (5-year) — QFR-Guided vs Angiography-Guided PCI: Sustained Benefit at 5 Years
Zhang H et al. · J Am Coll Cardiol 2026 · source ↗

In the 5-year follow-up of the FAVOR III China randomised trial (patients with ≥1 intermediate coronary lesion 50–90% stenosis), MACE remained significantly lower with QFR guidance than angiography guidance (17.5% vs 21.1%; HR 0.80; 95% CI 0.69–0.92; P=0.002), driven by fewer myocardial infarctions (5.8% vs 9.0%; HR 0.63; 95% CI 0.49–0.80; P<0.0001) and ischaemia-driven revascularisations (9.6% vs 12.0%; HR 0.78; 95% CI 0.64–0.95; P=0.02); all-cause death did not differ. Landmark analysis showed that the benefit accrued predominantly within the first 2 years (HR 0.66; P<0.0001), with similar outcomes between 2 and 5 years (HR 0.90; P for interaction 0.001) — consistent with the mechanism being avoidance of unnecessary stenting rather than ongoing haemodynamic benefit, and further cementing QFR-guided deferral as the standard for angiographically intermediate lesions.

— — M-TEER Improves Forward Stroke Volume in Atrial But Not Ventricular Secondary MR
Grewe F et al. · Clin Res Cardiol 2026 · source ↗

In 103 patients with secondary MR undergoing M-TEER (ventricular n=60, atrial n=43), forward stroke volume remained unchanged in ventricular MR (49.4 ± 17.3 vs 49.5 ± 16.5 mL; P=0.960) but increased significantly in atrial MR (60.5 ± 21.7 vs 66.8 ± 19.6 mL; P=0.037) at 4 weeks. Atrial MR aetiology (OR 5.12; P=0.048) and lower baseline FSV (OR 0.36 per 10 mL/m²; P=0.002) independently predicted FSV improvement exceeding 10% on multivariable analysis. FSV improvement was not associated with reduced mortality or HF hospitalisation over a 2-year follow-up, suggesting that FSV response reflects haemodynamic adaptation rather than serving as a prognostic marker.

Brazilian TAVI Registry — Valve-in-Valve vs Native Valve TAVI: In-Hospital Outcomes From 25 Centres
Calomeni P et al. · Int J Cardiol 2026 · source ↗

In 3,194 patients from 25 Brazilian TAVI centres (2009–2021), 135 underwent ViV-TAVI; after propensity score matching (127 ViV vs 248 NV-TAVI), ViV patients had lower device success (71.7% vs 87.9%; OR 0.35; 95% CI 0.20–0.60; P<0.01) and lower permanent pacemaker implantation (4.7% vs 12.9%; OR 0.35; 95% CI 0.13–0.79; P<0.01) compared with NV-TAVI. Valve embolisation was more frequent in ViV-TAVI (3.9% vs 0.8%; OR 4.44; 95% CI 1.03–25.59; P=0.04). Major vascular complications, major bleeding, stroke, and in-hospital mortality did not differ significantly between groups, supporting ViV-TAVI as feasible but technically distinct from native-valve procedures.

HONEST — S-ICD Implantation Under Non-General Anaesthesia: Nationwide Cohort Outcomes
Donisi L et al. · Europace 2026 · source ↗

In the nationwide HONEST cohort (4,924 patients implanted with S-ICD in France 2012–2019), non-general anaesthesia (non-GA) was used in 1,041 patients (21.1%), increasing from 2.5% to 26.9% over the study period. Thirty-day complication rate was 3.4% in the non-GA group vs 3.7% in the GA group (P=0.85); at follow-up, rates of overall complications (4.29 vs 4.73 events per 100 person-years), reintervention, inappropriate shocks (2.88 vs 2.78), and all-cause death (2.93 vs 2.56) were similar (all P>0.1 by multivariate analysis). These data establish non-GA as a safe and increasingly adopted implantation approach for S-ICD, simplifying logistics and potentially expanding access.

— — Moderate AS Plus Concomitant MR: High-Risk Subgroup With Outcomes Comparable to Severe AS
Gabr EM et al. · JACC Cardiovasc Imaging 2026 · source ↗

In 742 patients with at least moderate AS on CMR, the 76 with concurrent moderate/severe MR had independently worse cardiovascular death or HF hospitalisation (adjusted HR 1.86; 95% CI 1.31–2.65; P<0.001) and high symptom burden (80.3% in NYHA class II–IV), with risk more pronounced in the low-flow subgroup (stroke volume index ≤35 mL/m²). Outcomes in moderate AS with moderate/severe MR were comparable to intervention-threshold severe AS populations. These data highlight a therapeutic gap and support studies of earlier intervention in this dual-valvular high-risk group.

— — IVUS-Guided Intracoronary Brachytherapy for In-Stent Restenosis: Outcomes Analysis
Kharsa C et al. · JACC Adv 2026 · source ↗

In 221 patients undergoing brachytherapy for in-stent restenosis (59.7% IVUS-guided), IVUS use was associated with larger assessed vessel diameter (3.6 ± 0.04 mm vs 3.3 ± 0.05 mm; P<0.001), more frequent advanced lesion preparation (49.2% vs 19.1%; P<0.001), and longer brachytherapy dwell time (332.2 ± 2.6 s vs 313.4 ± 4.2 s; P<0.001); at median follow-up of 717.9 days, all-cause mortality (8.3% vs 23.6%; P=0.03) and postprocedural MI (11.4% vs 25.8%; P=0.005) were lower in IVUS-guided cases, though Cox regression showed higher target lesion revascularisation in the IVUS group. These observational data support IVUS guidance in brachytherapy but confirm complex case mix confounding.

— — Concomitant LAAO and AF Ablation: Propensity-Matched Safety Analysis
Sawalha K et al. · JACC Adv 2026 · source ↗

In a propensity-matched analysis of 1,899 patients per group from the TriNetX US network, concomitant AF ablation at the time of LAAO was not associated with increased adverse events at 90 days (mortality 0.6% vs 0.6%; HR 1.00; 95% CI 0.43–2.32) or 1 year (mortality 1.4% vs 1.5%; HR 0.97; 95% CI 0.58–1.63); rates of stroke, device leak, and device-related thrombus were comparable at both time points. These large real-world data support the procedural safety of a combined approach in appropriately selected patients, though randomised data on long-term efficacy outcomes remain needed.

Worth knowing

— — IVUS-Guided vs Angiography-Guided PCI for Unprotected Left Main Disease: RCT Meta-Analysis
Elhakim A et al. · Clin Cardiol 2026 · source ↗

Meta-analysis of 4 RCTs (2,278 patients with unprotected left main coronary artery disease) found that IVUS-guided PCI was associated with numerically lower risks of all-cause death, cardiac death, and target lesion revascularisation compared with angiography guidance, though none of the differences reached statistical significance; myocardial infarction and stent thrombosis were similar between groups. The consistently favourable numerical trend across multiple endpoints and RCTs justifies powered future trials to determine whether IVUS superiority varies by prior MI status or LVEF.

COMPARE-TAVI 1 — Inhospital, Subacute, and Elective TAVI: Comparable 1-Year Outcomes Across Urgency Strata
Thim T et al. · Eur Heart J Open 2026 · source ↗

In 1,030 TAVI patients from COMPARE-TAVI 1 (90% elective, 6.1% subacute, 3.9% inhospital), procedural characteristics and 1-year outcomes were comparable across groups: mortality at 1 year was 5.8%, 6.3%, and 5.0% respectively; stroke rates were 4.5%, 7.9%, and 7.5%; and new pacemaker rates were 16%, 25%, and 19%. Elective, subacute, and inhospital patients with LVEF ≤40% improved their LVEF from baseline values of 33%, 33%, and 28% to 54%, 53%, and 49% by 1 year, with no statistically significant differences between groups. These findings support TAVI as a viable therapeutic option even in less-elective clinical scenarios.

— — TAVR in Latin America: 12-Year Single-Centre VARC-3 Outcomes Report
Rojas-Perdomo JD et al. · Catheter Cardiovasc Interv 2026 · source ↗

Among 134 consecutive TAVR patients at an established Colombian programme (2012–2023; median age 80 years; 52.2% female), technical success was 95.5% and device success at 30 days was 81.3% by VARC-3 criteria; 30-day mortality was 7.4%, with acute kidney injury (19.4%), permanent pacemaker implantation (18.7%), and vascular complications (12.7%) as the most common adverse events. Exploratory multivariable analysis identified diabetes mellitus as a risk factor for adverse outcomes. These real-world Latin American data demonstrate feasibility of a high-fidelity TAVI programme and the importance of VARC-3 standardisation for international outcome comparison.

Fuwai PCI Registry — Sex Differences in Outcomes of PCI for Moderate-to-Severe Coronary Calcification
Lin Z et al. · Catheter Cardiovasc Interv 2026 · source ↗

In 10,681 consecutive PCI patients, 18.24% had moderate-to-severe coronary artery calcification (MSCAC); among those with MSCAC, women had a higher adjusted risk of MACCE than men (HR 2.89; 95% CI 1.56–5.34), primarily from cardiovascular death and stroke. After age 62, women showed higher MSCAC prevalence than men (adjusted OR 1.24; 95% CI 1.01–1.52), while before age 62 men predominated. Sex-specific risk stratification and aggressive calcium modification may be particularly important in women with calcified coronary disease.

— — eGFR-Specific Troponin Thresholds Improve AMI Diagnosis in Renal Impairment
Collard D et al. · Heart 2026 · source ↗

In 221,175 acute hospital presentations (London, 2010–2017), diagnostic performance of unadjusted troponin for AMI declined with worsening renal function (C-statistic 0.93 for eGFR >90 vs 0.81 for eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73 m²), with false positive rates of 68–93% at conventional 14 ng/L hs-TnT cut-off for eGFRs of 15–60. Proposed eGFR-specific thresholds of 73, 112, and 184 ng/L (for eGFR 45–60, 30–45, 15–30 respectively) would reduce false positives to 15% while maintaining detection rates of 68–73%. These data support consideration of renal-adjusted troponin thresholds in ACS triage for patients with CKD.

— — Medial vs Intimal Calcification: Inverse Spatial Relationship in Lower Extremity Artery Disease
Kato T et al. · JACC Adv 2026 · source ↗

Systematic histopathological analysis of 3,566 cross-sections from 125 arterial segments in 58 patients revealed medial artery calcification in 91.2% of segments with an inverse correlation with intimal calcification arcs (r=−0.47; P<0.0001) and minimal circumferential overlap. MAC was more extensive below-the-knee while intimal atherosclerotic calcification predominated above-the-knee. Advanced MAC was associated with non-atherosclerotic intima, potentially explaining the limited response of below-the-knee lesions to conventional endovascular approaches and suggesting distinct therapeutic targets.

MIRON-PREDICT — Pre-Reperfusion Prediction of Intramyocardial Haemorrhage in STEMI Using Explainable AI
Youssef K et al. · JACC Adv 2026 · source ↗

In 288 STEMI patients (252 development, 36 validation), a 3-variable explainable AI model (coronary collaterals, degree of occlusion, sum ST-score) predicted pre-reperfusion intramyocardial haemorrhage with 84.9% accuracy, 82.3% sensitivity, and 87.3% specificity, confirmed in the validation cohort (83.3% accuracy). CMR 48–72 hours post-PCI identified IMH in 142/288 patients. This real-time bedside model may identify patients for targeted cardioprotective strategies before reperfusion injury occurs.

— — Palliative Care Timing After AMI Cardiogenic Shock: Earlier Referral Reduces Hospital and ICU Death
Sterling LH et al. · JACC Adv 2026 · source ↗

In 3,881 AMI-CS survivors in Ontario who died after discharge (median survival 1,096 days), 64% received palliative care in the final year of life, most commonly initiated in the last 14 days (47.7%); earlier palliative care referral was associated with reduced risk of dying in hospital (adjusted OR 0.50; 95% CI 0.42–0.65) and in the ICU (adjusted OR 0.34; 95% CI 0.26–0.45). Most patients (54.1%) died in acute care regardless of palliative care receipt. These data support earlier, proactive palliative care integration in cardiogenic shock survivor pathways to reduce ICU-intensive end-of-life trajectories.

YOUNG-AF — Young-Onset Atrial Fibrillation: High Rate of Adverse Events and Role of LGE-CMR
D'Elia NW et al. · JACC Clin Electrophysiol 2026 · source ↗

In 203 patients with AF diagnosed at ≤45 years (mean age 34.8 years, 79% male) followed for a median of 4.9 years, MACCE occurred in 28% and new LV dysfunction developed in 13%; genetic, alcohol, or structural heart disease-associated AF carried the highest event burden. Catheter ablation was performed in 88.7% with 39% recurrence; late gadolinium enhancement on CMR predicted both MACCE and shorter recurrence-free survival (P<0.01 for both). These data challenge the common assumption that young-onset AF is benign and support CMR and genetic evaluation as standard workup in this population.

Landmark Revisited

COAPT — Transcatheter Mitral-Valve Repair in Patients with Heart Failure
Stone GW, Lindenfeld J, Abraham WT et al. (COAPT Investigators) · N Engl J Med 2018 · primary source ↗
Structured abstract
Background
Secondary (functional) mitral regurgitation in heart failure results from adverse LV remodelling and annular dilatation; it persists in approximately half of patients despite maximally tolerated GDMT and independently predicts hospitalisation and death. Whether adding transcatheter edge-to-edge repair to optimised GDMT could improve outcomes in this population was untested in a controlled trial.
Methods
Prospective, multicentre, randomised, open-label, blinded-endpoint (PROBE) trial at 78 sites in the US and Canada. Eligible patients had moderate-to-severe or severe secondary MR (grade 3+ or 4+), symptomatic HF (NYHA class II–IV), LVEF 20–50%, and echocardiographically eligible mitral anatomy with LV dimensions within protocol limits, having remained symptomatic despite maximally tolerated GDMT. 614 patients were randomised 1:1 to MitraClip transcatheter mitral repair plus GDMT vs GDMT alone. Co-primary endpoints were the annualised rate of all HF hospitalisations at 24 months and all-cause mortality at 24 months, tested hierarchically. A device safety endpoint assessed freedom from device-related complications at 12 months against a prespecified performance goal of 88.0%.
Key results
Annualised HF hospitalisation rate at 24 months: 35.8% per patient-year (device group) vs 67.9% per patient-year (control group) — HR 0.53; 95% CI 0.40–0.70; P<0.001. All-cause mortality at 24 months: 29.1% vs 46.1% — HR 0.62; 95% CI 0.46–0.82; P<0.001. Freedom from device-related complications at 12 months: 96.6% (lower 95% CI 94.8%; P<0.001 vs the prespecified 88.0% performance goal).
Conclusion
In patients with HF and secondary MR remaining symptomatic on maximally tolerated GDMT, MitraClip transcatheter repair substantially reduced HF hospitalisation and all-cause mortality at 24 months. The strict eligibility criteria — particularly GDMT optimisation before enrolment and the MR quantification thresholds — were central to achieving the benefit, explaining divergence from the contemporaneous neutral MITRA-FR trial.
The question & the verdict

COAPT enrolled 614 patients at 78 US and Canadian sites who had heart failure and moderate-to-severe or severe secondary mitral regurgitation remaining symptomatic despite maximal doses of guideline-directed medical therapy. Patients were randomised to transcatheter mitral-valve repair (MitraClip) plus medical therapy or medical therapy alone.

The annualised rate of all hospitalisations for heart failure within 24 months was 35.8% per patient-year in the device group vs 67.9% per patient-year in the control group (hazard ratio 0.53; 95% CI 0.40–0.70; P<0.001). Death from any cause within 24 months occurred in 29.1% of device patients vs 46.1% of controls (hazard ratio 0.62; 95% CI 0.46–0.82; P<0.001). The rate of freedom from device-related complications at 12 months was 96.6% (lower 95% confidence limit 94.8%; P<0.001 vs the prespecified objective performance goal of 88.0%).

Statistical appraisal

COAPT used a PROBE design (prospective randomised open-label blinded endpoint). The primary endpoint was annualised HF hospitalisation rate at 24 months, a recurrent-events approach appropriate for the frequent hospitaliser phenotype. The absolute mortality benefit of 17% at 24 months is among the largest in any heart failure device trial and raised immediate questions about external validity. The key design feature — rigorous GDMT optimisation before randomisation — excluded patients likely to respond to medical therapy intensification alone, enriching for device-responsive biology. This explains, in part, why the contemporaneous MITRA-FR trial (less stringent GDMT pre-enrolment and broader MR quantification criteria) showed neutral results. The "proportionate MR" concept — where MR severity is disproportionate to LV dilation and more likely to respond to valve repair — has since become central to patient selection, enshrined in ESC 2021 valve guidelines (Class IIa for M-TEER in secondary MR meeting eligibility criteria).

Subsequent substudies & follow-ups
  • Health status substudy — Arnold SV et al. J Am Coll Cardiol 2019 (PMID 30894288): KCCQ-OS between-group difference was 12.8 points at 24 months (95% CI 7.5–18.2; P<0.001); parallel gains in SF-36 physical and mental scores confirmed patient-perceived benefit tracks the hard-endpoint reduction.
  • Proportionate vs disproportionate MR analysis — Grayburn PA et al. JACC Cardiovasc Imaging 2019 (PMID 30553663): Patients with "disproportionate" secondary MR — where MR severity exceeds what the degree of LV dilation would predict — showed greater device benefit, providing the mechanistic framework now embedded in ESC and ACC/AHA guideline eligibility criteria for M-TEER.
  • 3-year outcomes — Mack MJ et al. JACC 2021 (PMID 33632476): The reductions in HF hospitalisation and all-cause mortality observed at 24 months were maintained at 3 years (all-cause death 42.8% vs 55.5%; P=0.001), confirming that the COAPT benefit is durable rather than reflecting a short-term honeymoon effect.
Why it still matters: COAPT established that transcatheter edge-to-edge repair substantially reduces HF hospitalisation and all-cause mortality in well-selected patients with functional MR, making patient selection and GDMT optimisation — not the device alone — the central clinical skills. This week's GeminiOne-DMR trial results (Deep Dive 3) and M-TEER forward stroke volume analysis (Tier 2) directly extend COAPT's legacy into new device platforms and haemodynamic phenotyping, underscoring the ongoing importance of comprehensive valve and ventricular assessment before TEER.
